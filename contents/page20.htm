WHEN THE BEST LAID PLANS GO AWRY.

Enki, the newly appointed Lord of Lords, walked back and forth before the assembled Sorcerers that had gathered at her imperial request. He/She was almost entirely male now because her male aspect commanded more respect from the other Sorcerers.

"Ibis, what are we going to do? Everything is going wrong. He...everything...he's ruining...everything."

Thoth tore his eyes from the scene inside the great table. He had been doing nothing but trying to figure out what to do since Marduk's betrayal. He didn't have an answer yesterday and resented like hell being called on the spot again, today--as if he had some kind of power they didn't all have besides more smarts. Smarts can't put a spaceship together from thin air.

Marduk was ruining everything alright. The first Person the Noids was supposed meet was him, Thoth, and then the rest of these Fools. Now the Noids will have to be destroyed and everything will have to be done all over again. By the time a ship is ready to get him to earth there will be millions of Noids. But so be it. They are bound to worship Marduk, now, and there's nothing to be done with them. At least nothing he has thought of yet. He'd really hate to destroy them--go through the whole long process over again.

'Well...?"

Thoth wrestled down the urge to answer her with the honesty of his irritation. But she was Eeen, now, and he was in an entirely different state of mind. He no longer felt like sparring with fools. All he wanted was to get to earth before Marduk built something with the Igigis and Noids that the Guild would not be able to over come.

Progress with the ship was going slower than he thought it would. Two hundred years gone by, and they haven't even gotten pass the stage of training the Igigis to do the simplest technological jobs. None of these fools in the Guild were any help. If he had even suspected that Marduk would betray them so ruthlessly, he would have kept some of his Igigi Scientists and some of the skilled laborers alive. Of course, on the new earth, the Guild wanted nothing but unthinking beasts who would worship and slave for them without the problems of rebellion. That's why the Noids were created. But it was too late for ifs, and buts. Marduk has most effectively ruined four thousand years of meticulous planning.

Thoth answered Ki:

"I'm working on it Lord Ki. I know this is the last thing you want to hear but I need skilled workers and meticulous Thinkers and I just don't have any. Some of the Igigis show signs of a natural talent in the fields we need but you know what has been done to their psyches. They will never think beyond certain limits. It's going to take time and a new generation of abds to complete the space program ."

The serpent seed exploded.

"Don't thou understand, fool? Everything hath changed! We are running out of time!"

"I understand very well, Lord Ki."

Erra, who was watching them both with growing enjoyment, deflated at Thoth's calm response to being called a fool. He wanted to see a fight. A fight that was sure to leave Thoth humiliated and he an opportunity to climb the ladder back up to his proper place of respect.

"Ibis..."

Thoth turned to Inanna in surprise. It has been a long time since he'd last heard her voice in Council.

"What if instead of just concentrating on building a ship, we developed a means to project our thoughts to earth?”

Thoth was already mentally running with the idea before Inanna finished her sentence. A great grin spread across his face as he listened to her expand on her idea.

"That way, we can influence the Noids without physically being there."

Nergal grasped the scope of Inanna's theme as quickly as Thoth did.

"Can it be done, Ibis? Can it?" His loud voice came out like a thunderous wind, -- so great was his excitement he shifted between Saurian and WoManoid form three times before getting control of his will.

Ishkur, Esergikel, Ki, Erra, Apep, and Hadad weren't quite sure what Inanna's idea meant but being filled with the atoms of a hive collective, they internalized Thoth's and Nergal's fervor and convulsed accordingly.

Thoth felt like an Igigi that was bleeding because of the way they were all shape shifting and crowding in on him.

Onanes stared at his last Generation daughter, morosely. He had taken steps to help his first Generation grandson Marduk, and now Inanna had just handed the advantage back to the Guild. He felt like crying. In fact, he always felt like crying. He couldn't sleep for crying; couldn't eat for crying. Memories...old, dark haunting memories that never gave him a moment's rest, tormented him day and night. He had long ago given up trying to kill himself. He was growing older and feebler everyday. Everything on his body ached like an infested tooth but nothing hurt as much as his soul and he--who had for so long played the role of Supreme Being--began to pray for death. But they won't let him die. They won't let him sleep for a moment-- much less forever.

The dead Ones.

He had forgotten so much, but what he remembered was enough to fill a library. He looked at his seed daughter, Inanna--at the path she was taking. He cried big, silent tears that drowned him inside, but never let him die. Everyday since Atlan was destroyed, he felt like a character in a dream. Did he really destroy his own children? He had taken part in desolating many civilizations, but he had never destroyed one filled with his own seeds; so why does his mind continuously accuse him with such vivid pictures? He never did such things to his children...did he? He couldn't remember but he wouldn't...would he? They lie...The dead Ones. They lie.

He tried to remember backwards in time...To an Age long passed. He faintly recalled being drawn to something against his will. What was it? What...?

He had to leave, find a dark place--a lonely, secluded place, so he could let the tears that were drowning him inside out. It had to some place far away; otherwise the whole Universe will hear him weeping forever.
